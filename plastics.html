The Dangers of Plastic



These before and after pictures of our Earth's oceans helps to descibe the impact that humans are having on our oceans. Specifically, this page
we are going to be focused on the impact the plastic has on our environment. Every aspect of plastic has a harmful impact on the environment: the creation of
plastics, its usage, and its disposal all harms either certain organisms (like humans and animals) or the Earth.

Doesn't Break Down

Plastics are made to last forever. Plastic is not
biodegradabe; it is photodegradable, meaning that plastic
never disappears, but instead just breaks up into smaller
smaller pieces called microplastics. The small plastic
fragments are easily absorbed by water and soil and
easily digested by people and animals.

Threatens Wildlife

Every square mile of ocean has 46,000 pieces of plastic
floating on it. This is why over a million sea birds,
whales, seals, dolphins, sea turtles, and more die from
the plastic debris that is ending up in our oceans.
Aquatic animals become entangled in plastic or they eat it
or mistake it for food and feed it to their young. In our
oceans, plastic debris outweighs zooplankton by a ratio
of 36-to-1.

Threatens Humans

Chemicals added to plastics are absorbed by humans and
are now found in the blood and tissue of everybody.
Exposure to these chemicals such as phthalates, which are found
in food packaging and wall coverings, and bisphenol A (BPA),
which is found in water bottles and beverage cans, can leach into
food and drink and poison humans, causing ailments such as cancer
birth defects, and hormone disruption.

Waste Storage

The plastic industry produces 400 million tons of plastic a year,
but only 9% gets recycled. Many plastic products are sitting
in landfills, burned, or thrown into the ocean. Heavily popular
today is single use plastic products, which unnecessarily produces
an abundant amount of trash. The toxic chemicals present within
the plastics many times leach and soak into the surrounding soil,
bodies of water, or groundwater supplies.

Supports Fossil Fuel Industry

The United States production of plastic alone uses 331 billion
barrels of petroleum, which is 5% of the national consumption
of petroleum. Petroleum has very harmful effects, both from mining
it and from burning it to make electricity. For more information
about petroleum, go to our Oil Energy page

Cost Billions

Many different industries suffer and are costed billions of dollars
because of plastic pollution. Tourism, recreation, business, the
health of humans, animals, fish and birds are all harmed and
destroyed because of the plastic that humans manufacture and
improperly dispose of. To marine environmentments alone, plastic
costs $13 billion in economic damage each year.



The Great Pacific Garbage Patch

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch is a collection of marine debris that is
floating in the middle of the Pacific Ocean. The litter is held withing one
area because of an ocean gyre, which is a system of circular currents.
Much of the trash that gets trapped is not biodegradble; instead most of
the trash breaks up into microplastics. Many people imagine the Great
Pacific Garbage Patch as a mile high pile of trash. It is actually, a
large part of the ocean that holds hundreds of thousands of pieces of
microplastics. Many marine animals end up ingesting these pieces of
plastics and then humans end up eating these animals.

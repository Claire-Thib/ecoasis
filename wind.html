Wind Turbines


Wind energy is obtained by capturing wind with wind turbines and converting it into electrical energy. Wind power is actually the second fastest growing source of energy. Europe is currently leading the world in the age of wind energy, with places like Germany and Spain producing three-fourths of the global wind-generated power. Wind turbines are larger than ever and have even bigger blades, meaning that the current wind turbines can tap into the stronger, more reliable, and less turbulent winds found at higher altitudes

Cons

• Steady winds are needed to sustain the turbines
• Backup systems needed when winds are low
• Plastic components produced from oil (supporting the petroleum industry)
• Environmental costs not included in market price
• High land use needed for wind turbines
• Pollutes the visual aesthetics for the surrounding people
• Noise for surrounding people when located in a populated area
• Can kill birds and interfere with flight paths of migratory birds

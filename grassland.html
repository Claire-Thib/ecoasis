Grasslands

Tropical Grassland

Tropical Grasslands are a terrestrial biome that has vast open spaces and consists of scattered small shrubs and trees. They also have a handful of the world's most recognizable species such as lions, cheetahs, hyenas, zebras, gazelles, elephants, giraffes, wildebeests and warthogs. During the winter tropical grasslands are very dry with a temperature range of 68 to 78˚F. All of the rainfall happens in the summer with a temperature of 68 to 86˚F.

Temperate Grassland

Temperate Grasslands are areas of open grassland with a few trees. They're located in colder climate regions. In the winter in certain places temperatures can get lower than 0˚F and in the summer it can reach above 90˚F. On average every year temperate grasslands recieve around 20-35 inches of rainfall. Tornadoes, blizzards, and fires are three natural factors that impact temperate grasslands.

Deserts

Hot and Dry Deserts

Hot and Dry Deserts have very high temperatures year-round, having very low humidity levels. Rainfall is very sparse and evaporation rates are very high. Plants in this desert include mostly small shrubs and short, woody trees. The animals include small nocturnal carnivores, reptiles, and birds, many of which hide during the day from the piercing sun and come out during the night to hunt or forage.

Semiarid Deserts

Semiarid deserts are deserts that are are normally hot and dry, but have a low concentration of rainfall in the winter. The spiny nature of many of the plants protect the crops from the hazardous environment that is the Semi-arid desert. Many animals find protection underground to help them protect themselves from the oppressive heat that is present during the day.

Coastal Deserts

Coastal Deserts are moderately cool to warm and are located near the coast. The soil is usually fine-textured and contains a moderately high salt-content. Many of the plants have large root systems near the surface so they can absorb much of the moisture produced from rain showers.

Cold Deserts

Cold Deserts have extremely cold winters, warm or hot summers, and low precipitation levels. Specific plants and animals have certain adaptation that help them stay cool and have enough water to survive. The winters receive a lot of slow and the plants are widely scattered in the area.
